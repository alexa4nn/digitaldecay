Week 5: Metadata for Loss, Failure + Uncertainty

Overview

This week connects research, archives, and interface. We begin by following a rabbit hole—moving through links, images, and references—to gather material. This process produces an archive shaped by curiosity, selection, and omission. We then shift to metadata, asking how to describe what is missing, unclear, or unresolved. Finally, we introduce HTML and CSS as tools for structuring and displaying that archive as a webpage.

Activity

Rabbit Hole Archive + Metadata of Absence

Begin with a single entry point—a keyword, image, link, or reference—and follow connections as they emerge. Collect 20–50 items as you move: links, images, screenshots, text fragments, or notes. Organize what you gather into a simple archive using Are.na Then write metadata that describes not only what is present, but what is missing, unclear, or unresolved in the descriptions. Use Are.na API to display your archive as a webpage, making the metadata visible alongside the items.

CSS Workshop

We will introduce CSS as a way of shaping a wensite's appearance. Edit your archive webpages with CSS. The goal is not technical complexity, but clarity—how to make your archive visible, and how to represent absence within it.

Assignment: Archive of Digital Loss

Organize your collected materials into your current archive site and add some styling.

The Walk + Carrier Bag

Conduct an internet walk (minimum 1 hour) following a question-path about digital loss. Submit a path map, a log of sites visited, and a collected folder of materials including accessible, degraded, and absent objects.

The Schema

Design a metadata schema for the full range of your carrier bag. Include Dublin Core fields plus at least 5 custom fields for loss, uncertainty, and failed access. For each custom field, include: name, definition, value type, and one example from your walk.

Three Records

Apply your schema to three items: one accessible object, one degraded object, and one object known only from a trace. Complete every field honestly. Flag speculation and admit unknowns.

Reflection

Write a 400-word reflection drawing on Burke, Chang + Tjalve, Gilliland, and Edwards. Consider: what did the walk produce that a search would not? How does the path become part of the archive? What does it mean to write a record for an absence?
