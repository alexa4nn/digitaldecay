Week 2: Behaviors + Dependencies + Systems

Overview

This week shifts from thinking about digital artworks as stable objects to understanding them as behaviors that unfold through technical systems. We will examine how software, hardware, operating systems, browsers, and networks shape how a work appears, functions, and changes over time. Rather than treating infrastructure as background, we will consider how dependency makes digital works legible and vulnerable.

Themes

  • artworks as behaviors
  • dependency
  • technical systems
  • infrastructure visibility

In Class

  • lecture on artworks as systems rather than objects
  • discussion of variable media and performative materiality
  • case studies of artworks dependent on specific technical environments
  • workshop on mapping dependencies

Key Questions

  • What does a digital artwork require in order to function?
  • When those systems change, does the artwork become a different work?
  • Which dependencies are visible, and which remain hidden?
  • How do failures make infrastructures legible?

Activity

Dependency Mapping

Choose a digital artwork and identify the systems it depends on: software, hardware, file formats, platforms, operating systems, browsers, plugins, and display conditions. Map those dependencies visually and consider which are most vulnerable to change or obsolescence.

Workshop Steps

  • select a digital or time-based artwork
  • list its required software and hardware
  • identify platform and network dependencies
  • note version-specific or historical requirements
  • consider what happens if one dependency disappears

Assignment

Dependency Map + Short Commentary

Create a dependency map for one artwork and write a short commentary explaining which systems are most essential, which are most fragile, and how those systems shape the work’s meaning or experience. Which dependencies are most critical? Which are most vulnerable to change? How do those systems shape the work’s meaning or experience?
